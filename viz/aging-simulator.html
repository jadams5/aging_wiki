Aging causal-graph simulator (v0.5)

An interactive map of how the drivers of human aging connect — from everyday inputs (diet, activity, smoking, sleep) through clinical risk factors (cholesterol, blood pressure, blood sugar) and the molecular hallmarks of aging, down to the diseases people actually die of. Adjust a lifestyle factor, a lab value, or a hypothetical intervention and watch life expectancy, the projected cause-of-death breakdown, and each factor's trajectory over your lifespan respond. Mortality is modelled as sex-specific competing risks calibrated to open population data (CDC WONDER mortality, NHANES, and published dose-response studies): accumulated damage at each node raises the risk of the disease it feeds, and an intervention helps by slowing that accumulation rather than by resetting the clock to a given age. Hover the small (i) icons throughout for the assumptions and math behind any number. This is an exploratory sensitivity tool — not a prediction and not medical advice.
⚠ This is an experiment, not a health tool. It is built on real, open-access population data (CDC WONDER, NHANES, published dose-response literature), but it is a simplified sensitivity explorer for understanding how causal factors interact — not a personal predictor and not a source of actionable medical insight. Do not use any output to make health, lifestyle, or treatment decisions. Consult a qualified clinician for those.
