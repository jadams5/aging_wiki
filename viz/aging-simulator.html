Aging causal-graph simulator (v0.4)

Illustrative sensitivity model — parameters are mostly illustrative (see causal-graph-parameters.md). v0.4 — sex-specific competing hazards (CDC WONDER 2022); no sex scalar — female CVD-onset delay & male external excess emerge from data. Old-age escalation is now burden-driven (no age-keyed Gompertz factor): cause hazards use an odds link Rmax·B/(1−B) on reserve-depletion burden, so interventions bend the >90 mortality curve. Mortality sums a per-sex extrinsic channel (accidents / suicide / homicide, scaled by the Lifestyle slider) and a per-sex intrinsic bracket of named CDC causes + a residual, amplified by a sarcopenia-driven frailty multiplier. Coupling propagates intervention relief through the graph as a bounded algebraic fixed point. The B-layer (Lifestyle & environment + Labs) drives emergent mediator values (LDL / SBP / HbA1c) and their clean mediator→cause / direct exogenous→cause mortality multipliers. All numbers come from model/engine.mjs (the single source of truth). Not prediction, not medical advice.
⚠ This is an experiment, not a health tool. It is built on real, open-access population data (CDC WONDER, NHANES, published dose-response literature), but it is a simplified sensitivity explorer for understanding how causal factors interact — not a personal predictor and not a source of actionable medical insight. Do not use any output to make health, lifestyle, or treatment decisions. Consult a qualified clinician for those.
