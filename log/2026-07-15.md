# 2026-07-15

## Ingest — vegetarian diet and all-cause mortality

User-requested review of whether a vegetarian or vegan diet reduces all-cause mortality relative to a meat-inclusive diet after accounting for saturated/unsaturated fat and other dietary factors.

- Added [[vegetarian-diet]] as the canonical intervention page.
- Wiki-first search reviewed [[mediterranean-diet]], [[dietary-fat-quality]], and [[protein-intake]].
- Independent RCT and cohort evidence reviews searched literature through 2026-07-15.
- No mortality-powered RCT was found that compares a nutritionally optimized vegetarian/vegan diet with an otherwise equivalent meat-inclusive diet.
- Direct no-meat RCTs were classified as surrogate-endpoint evidence; long dietary-endpoint RCTs were kept separate because they test multi-component, meat-inclusive patterns.
- Cohort findings were separated by processed meat, unprocessed red meat, poultry, and fish, with statistical fat adjustment explicitly distinguished from experimental control.
- Propagated a comparison link to [[mediterranean-diet]].
- Verification corrected NEW Soul's primary outcomes to no significant between-group difference, narrowed CARDIVEG to the published first three-month phase, and corrected the EPIC-Oxford fish/vegetarian/vegan mortality estimates.
- Supersession search found a consistent 2026 observational substitution meta-analysis (nine cohorts; n=1,036,799; all-cause mortality HR 0.91, 95% CI 0.87–0.94; I²=77%), which was added with its observational limitation.
- Corrected the Cochrane 2020 `pub3` PMID pairing on [[dietary-fat-quality]] from 32428300 to 32827219.

Verification status: `verified: true` with explicit partial scope. Primary full texts were checked where accessible; CARDIVEG, Ornish, CORDIOPREV, Steen 2025, and Barrantes-Espinola 2026 were checked against publisher/PubMed records without an independently accessible full-text copy. `clinical-trials-active` remains unpopulated.
